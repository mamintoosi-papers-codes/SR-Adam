% SR-Adam Parameter Grouping and Application Analysis

For SimpleCNN, SR-Adam applies Stein-rule shrinkage \textbf{exclusively to convolutional layers}, while standard Adam is used for fully-connected layers and bias terms. Table \ref{tab:sradam_grouping} details the parameter groups:

\begin{table}[t]
  \centering
  \caption{SR-Adam parameter grouping in SimpleCNN: Stein-rule applied only to convolutional layer weights.}
  \label{tab:sradam_grouping}
  \begin{tabular}{l r r}
    \toprule
    Layer Group & Parameters & Stein-Rule \\
    \midrule
    Conv2d layers (weights + bias) & 18,528 & \textbf{Yes} \\
    \quad Conv2d-1: 3×32×3×3 + 32 & 896 & Yes \\
    \quad Conv2d-2: 32×64×3×3 + 64 & 18,496 & Yes \\
    Fully-connected layers (Linear-1, Linear-2) & 525,706 & No \\
    \quad Linear-1: 4096×128 + 128 & 524,416 & No \\
    \quad Linear-2: 128×10 + 10 & 1,290 & No \\
    \midrule
    \textbf{Total} & \textbf{545,098} & — \\
    \textbf{Stein-rule coverage} & \textbf{3.4\%} & — \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

The rationale for this selective application is to focus shrinkage on layers with high-dimensional feature maps where the Stein-rule estimator is most effective. Fully-connected layers are updated using standard adaptive moments (bias correction via Adam).
